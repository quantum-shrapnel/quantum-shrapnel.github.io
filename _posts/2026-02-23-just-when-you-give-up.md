LinkedIn gave me back my account! And I didn't have to give up my government ID! Huzzah! The support person I got ahold of was lovely and I appreciate them unblocking me (they didn't have to do that).

Although I must ask - if my ID wasn't central to verifying my information and gaining access to my account, then why ask for it at all? I am growing concerned with how comfortable companies are getting demanding PII to access their products. LinkedIn makes sense on *some* level, as you are supposed to represent yourself professionally. Even then there are other routes they could take before reaching for a government-issued ID. 

I don't even think it will be an effective strategy to prevent fraud. AI makes spoofing faces and documents trivial, and I sincerely doubt they are being reviewed under intense scrutiny. In the end, the people who engage earnestly with the system are the ones who suffer from it.
